Game Design Document

LYFE

A shared, location-based life simulation layered over the real world.
Version 0.1  •  Concept Stage
"It's just life."
▶ Play the Prototype

1. Elevator Pitch

Lyfe is a shared, location-based life simulation layered over the real world. Players walk through their actual neighborhoods to interact with AI-driven characters — called Sims — who live, age, work, fall in love, and raise families on a shared map. Unlike other AR games, the point isn't to catch something. The point is to go outside, show up, and build relationships over time.

CORE LOOP
Walk somewhere real → interact with a Sim → relationship evolves → the world remembers you were there.

Lyfe is designed for the generation that grew up going to friends' houses on foot — and stopped. It gamifies the act of showing up.

2. The World

2.1 The Map

The game world is built on OpenStreetMap data — free, open-source, and detailed enough to show individual houses, sidewalks, parks, and businesses. A digital layer sits on top of this map, populated with Sim homes, schedules, locations, and activity markers.

  • No Google Maps API dependency — fully open source stack
  • Streets, sidewalks, buildings, and landmarks are all rendered and walkable
  • Players see their real neighborhood, with Sim data overlaid as a second layer

2.2 World Generation (The Chunk System)

The world only exists where players have explored. This solves the empty-world problem for new users and keeps server load manageable.

  • On first login, 20–50 Sims are seeded within walking distance of the player
  • As a player walks into unexplored areas, new Sims spawn (approx. 3 per chunk entered)
  • Sims repopulate the map organically through the generational system (see Section 4)
  • All players share the same world — Sims are not private to any one account
DESIGN NOTE
The shared world is what makes Lyfe social. Your friend can befriend the same Sim you know — and that Sim will remember both of you.

3. The Sims

3.1 What Is a Sim?

Each Sim is an AI character anchored to a real-world location. They have a home address on the map, a daily schedule, a personality trait, a career, and a social life. They exist whether or not any player is interacting with them.

3.2 Personality Traits

Every Sim is generated with one dominant personality trait that shapes how they speak, what activities they enjoy, and how relationships with them develop. Example traits:

TraitDescription
The OptimistFinds the bright side of everything. Loves outdoor activities. Hard to upset.
The CynicDry humor, takes time to warm up. Deeply loyal once trust is built.
The HomebodyRarely strays far. Rich inner world. Best conversations happen at their place.
The Social ButterflyKnows everyone. Popular. High demand — you may have competition.
The DreamerAlways starting new projects. Loves talking about ideas. Slightly chaotic.
The CaretakerNurturing, remembers details about you. Gets hurt if you disappear.

3.3 Schedules

Sims follow real daily schedules tied to in-game time. A Sim who works at the bakery is there mornings. A night-owl artist is home late. Players need to actually show up at the right time — just like real life.

  • Morning: Home or commuting to work
  • Afternoon: At their workplace, park, or running errands
  • Evening: Home, socializing, or at a local venue
  • Sims occasionally post "status updates" visible on the map, hinting at their location

4. The Generational System

This is Lyfe's most original mechanic. Sims are not static — they live full lives, and the world evolves as generations turn over.

4.1 The Sim Lifecycle

Life StageGameplay Behavior
Childhood (0–12)Exists in the world but not interactable. Visible at family dinners, parks.
Teen (13–17)Interactable. Can be introduced by parent Sims. Impressionable — players can influence their path.
Young Adult (18–25)Most active social period. Dating, career starting, moving out.
Adult (26–55)Established. Career in full swing. Family of their own. Busier schedule.
Elder (56–70)Slowing down. Deep memories. Rich conversations. Eventually passes on.
DeathSim leaves behind offspring who carry their name and share some traits.

4.2 Offspring & Repopulation

When a Sim reaches adulthood, they may partner with another Sim and have children. These children grow up to become the next generation of interactable characters — keeping the world populated without developer intervention.

  • Children of Sims you knew will inherit partial memories of your relationship with their parent
  • Players can influence a teenage Sim's future (encourage college, a trade, travel)
  • Adult offspring may move to a new area of the map — expanding the world organically
  • A percentage stay near home; others relocate, seeding new neighborhoods
EMOTIONAL HOOK
You meet Marcus at 23. You watch him get married, have a daughter, grow old. His daughter Maya grows up knowing your name. That is a relationship no other game has.

5. Relationships & Bonding

5.1 The Relationship Meter

Every Sim tracks a relationship score with every player. This score is built by showing up — not by tapping. Distance traveled to interact carries real weight.

Relationship TierWhat Changes
StrangerJust met. Basic conversation only.
AcquaintanceA few visits. They remember your name. Light activities unlock.
FriendRegular contact. Deeper conversations. Invite to group activities.
Close FriendHigh trust. Vulnerable conversations. They'll introduce you to their network.
Best Friend / PartnerRare tier. Long history required. Unlocks the deepest story content.

5.2 Relationship Decay

Relationships are living things. Ghosting a Sim has consequences — modeled loosely on real life.

  • Short absence (1–2 weeks): Sim may mention they haven't seen you
  • Medium absence (1 month): Relationship score slowly dips
  • Long absence (2+ months): Sim moves on — new friends, new routines. Score resets partially.
  • Elder Sims are more forgiving of gaps. Teenage Sims feel it more.

5.3 Romantic Relationships

Romance is an optional layer — not a core objective. Players who want it can pursue it; players who don't will never be pushed toward it. Dating a Sim requires a close friendship first and evolves naturally through repeated visits and shared experiences.

6. Activities & Where You Go

What you do with a Sim depends entirely on where you physically go. The real world is the game board.

Real LocationActivity in Lyfe
ParkChat, play phone mini-games (frisbee, picnic), walk together
Movie TheaterWatch a film — Sim discusses the movie with you afterward using AI
Restaurant / CaféShared meal. Conversation-heavy. Relationship builds faster.
Bowling AlleyMini-game on phone + real bowling. Group activity — bring real friends too.
Sim's HomeDeepest conversations happen here. Meet their family. See their world.

KEY MECHANIC
Bring your real friends AND your Sims to the same place. Organize your crew to invite 3 Sims to bowling — your friends play real bowling, your Sims play the mini-game. Both exist in the same moment.

7. The Player Character & Career System

7.1 You Live a Life Too

Players are not observers — they have their own character who ages, makes career choices, and is shaped by their neighborhood. Inspired by BitLife, every few days the player receives life events and choices.

  • Career path is influenced by the Sims around you — a neighborhood full of lawyers nudges you toward law
  • You can choose to follow or resist those influences
  • Your career unlocks new types of Sims and activities
  • Player ages in sync with their Sims — you grow up together

7.2 Career Paths (Examples)

CareerWhat It Does
ChefUnlocked by knowing Sims who cook. Open a restaurant location on the map.
Lawyer / GovernmentUnlocked near legal/civic Sims. Influences local Sim laws and events.
ArtistUnlocked near creative Sims. Leaves permanent art on the map.
DoctorCan heal Sims, extend elder Sims' lifespans slightly.
TeacherInfluences teen Sims' development. Legacy mechanic.
EntrepreneurBuild businesses on the map that other Sims (and players) can visit.

8. Shared Memory & The Social Layer

Lyfe's AI remembers everything. This is what separates it from a single-player game.

  • A Sim who knows both you and your real-life friend will reference your friend in conversation
  • "Your friend Jamie came by last week — we talked about you actually."
  • Sims form opinions about players based on accumulated interactions
  • Popular Sims have high demand — multiple players may compete for their time
  • Sims gossip with other Sims, spreading your reputation through the world
VISION
Your real friends download Lyfe. You all know Marcus. Marcus becomes a shared character — a mutual friend who only exists because you both showed up. That's new.

9. Monetization

Lyfe's monetization is designed to never gate relationships or core gameplay behind payment. The world and its people are free.

Revenue StreamDescription
CosmeticsOutfits for your character, décor for your home, customization for Sim spaces you own.
Influence Radius ExpansionExpand the area around you where Sims spawn and events occur.
Business LicensesOpen a real-world-mapped business that Sims visit and generates passive social events.
Event HostingHost special events at real locations — invite Sims and real friends.
Legacy PassOptional subscription — deeper Sim memory, genealogy trees, archival of passed Sims.

What Lyfe will never sell: relationship shortcuts, pay-to-win Sim affection, or access to core map exploration.

11. Open Design Questions

These are the unresolved questions to address in the next design iteration:

  • Moderation: How do we handle player behavior toward Sims that is abusive or manipulative?
  • Privacy: How do we handle real home addresses being used as Sim home locations?
  • Pacing: How fast do Sims age? What is one in-game year in real time?
  • Onboarding: What is the ideal first 10-minute experience for a solo new player?
  • Content: Who writes the initial Sim personalities and dialogue seeds?
  • Cross-platform memory: What happens when a Sim's memory store gets very large?

12. Vision Statement

"Remember when you used to just walk to your friend's house?"

Lyfe is built for the 23-year-old who remembers being 16 and spending all day outside with friends — and wonders why that stopped. It doesn't replace real relationships. It creates a reason to go out and have them.

The AI characters are the excuse. The real world is the game. The people you walk with are the point.
